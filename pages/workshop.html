Outcomes from Transitions in Education and Work: Developing a Scalable Regional Approach

Pathways in Education and Work

This workshop will build on the existing work of Chapin Hall, Ohio State University, and the Coleridge Initiative to develop a common approach to measuring and describing how people move through education and workforce.

The workshop will be split in two segments: (i) a morning broadcast to set the context, and (ii) afternoon discussion groups for concrete next steps.

Morning session: context and community inputs

The morning session will feature 2-3 context setting speeches and a panel discussion of leading experts in the Education and Workforce fields. This session will be broadcast over WebEx - please register for the event here if you are interested in providing comments and feedback to the morning session.

Afternoon session: tactical next steps

A select group of 30 participants from state and local agencies in the Midwest will be split into smaller working session to define concrete next steps for a pilot including:

1. Three high level, multi-agency policy questions,
2. Potential data sources to use as a basis to answer those questions, and
3. Data analytics activities to develop standardized metrics in employment and education across jurisdictions
